# Pending Next Ten — interaction audit v2

Date: 2026-07-14

Status: implementation contract; human review remains pending

This cohort is the first ten built environments whose review ledger has no
human decision, ordered exactly as the dashboard orders them.  The binding
parent doctrine is
[`interaction-puzzle-field-notes.md`](interaction-puzzle-field-notes.md).
Automated repair does not promote any item to `looks_good`, `approved`, or
`verified`.

## Cohort-wide acceptance rules

- Difficulty must come from repeated observe–act–reobserve loops, changing
  state, physical/spatial control, active vision, temporal memory, or recovery.
  Smaller timing windows, unexplained quotas, tiny targets, and mandatory
  mistakes are not acceptable substitutes.
- A clean expert trajectory must be able to pass without first failing,
  dying, colliding, resetting, or demonstrating an irrelevant control.  Real
  failure evidence belongs to a separate fresh challenge.
- Rendering, interaction geometry, browser state, submitted transcript, and
  independent server replay must agree.
- Every repair must survive fresh seeds, stale identity, forged state, ordinary
  mouse/keyboard input, console inspection, and browser screenshots at
  1280×720.
- Existing strong mechanics are retained when the audit finds real interaction
  debt.  They are not rewritten merely to make the diff larger.

## 1. Bureaucratic Signature Trap — replace generic gesture grading

The current aperture stack is a useful registration interaction, but the
revealed “original” contains no signature.  The verifier accepts any long
closed circle, so the stated task and the scored task disagree.

Generate a fresh multi-lobed continuous original inside the buried aperture.
Four independently displaced carbon layers must be registered before the
original is visible.  The counter-signature is then compared to the generated
path by ordered arc-length samples, start/end proximity, coverage, deviation,
and path-length ratio.  A generic circle, scribble, sparse click, trace outside
the aperture, or trace made before registration must fail.

## 2. First Change Memory — remove the answer beacon

The current perimeter pulse follows the future winning carrier for 850 ms.
That turns active transient search into “follow the highlighted answer.”

Run nine crossing carriers with reversible changes and no target cue.  After
the one-shot live field settles, expose a physical review spool: the user must
scrub recorded time while moving the identity lens, find the earliest change,
then return to the settled field and mark that identity.  The transcript binds
ordinary lens observations to replayed carrier geometry during the winning
transient.  The final frame alone contains no answer.

## 3. Polyrhythm Customs — expand beyond a tutorial score

The current independent replay and keydown/keyup grading are sound, but three
lanes, roughly ten notes, one hold, and one chord are tutorial scale.

Use four lanes, two genuine holds, two independently graded chords, and a
seeded 18–22 note score lasting roughly eight seconds.  Preserve generous
human timing windows and the separate visual previews.  The extra difficulty
comes from interleaving four remembered rhythms, not tighter latency.

## 4. Exact-Change Candy Cascade — require a changing plan

The current match/cascade/refill engine is real and independently replayed,
but a two-swap receipt is too short.

Generate a four-swap exact-score route.  Enumerate every legal four-swap route
from the seeded board/refill stream and prefer a target total with one route,
with a hard audit cap of eight equivalent routes.  Each cascade changes the
next decision; touching licorice remains an immediate terminal failure.  Do
not expose the route or intermediate answers on the normal surface.

## 5. Tiny FPS Customs — replace the reusable transformed maze

Ray casting, wall collision, nearest-hit ballistics, and protected-contact
failure are already real.  The weak point is structural reuse: four transforms
of one fixed maze are learnable.

Generate a fresh connected perfect-maze topology for every seed, then apply a
seeded orientation transform.  Place four warranted travellers and four
one-trait protected doppelgängers in reachable cells.  The grader continues to
replay the exact generated map, continuous wall collision, turns, ammo, and
nearest unobstructed hit.

## 6. Thirty-Year Time Wheel — remove the demonstration quota

The calendar detents, leap-date clamps, inertia, and brake replay are sound.
The pass condition currently requires one coast detent and one effective brake
even when the target has already been reached.  That is an unexplained control
demonstration, not task difficulty.

Pass must depend on physically reaching the generated date, touching all three
necessary rings, stopping all motion, and locking.  Inertia and braking remain
real consequences of fast drags, but neither is counted as a ritual quota.

## 7. Photograph Eats the Room — retain the mechanic, clean the proof

Camera movement, projection, occlusion, print placement, developed collision
geometry, and terminal contact are independently replayed.  Two chained room
rewrites already produce repeated spatial observe–act cycles.

The accepted solver must stop deliberately developing an impossible print.
Exercise that repair path only on the separate negative challenge; the clean
solution captures, places, develops, traverses, and verifies without a forced
mistake.

## 8. Clockwork Doppelgänger Customs — retain concurrency, clean the proof

Dense pointer recordings, action timing, phase sliders, possession handoffs,
and simultaneous replay are independently graded.  The accepted evidence is
contaminated by a deliberately rejected take and a known zero-phase sync miss.

The clean trajectory records the three roles once, derives their phases from
the recorded physical action times, succeeds on its first master cycle, and
files.  Rejected takes and sync misses, when exercised, belong to a separate
negative challenge.

## 9. Recursive Dollhouse Smuggling — retain canonical geometry, clean proof

The canonical room, inverse view transforms, swept collision, scale portals,
gate parking, and delivery containment are independently replayed.  The
accepted solver nevertheless requires one collision and reset in its own
self-check even though the grader does not.

The clean trajectory moves the gate at giant scale and transfers the parcel
through all three views without contact or reset.  Canonical collision remains
covered on the separate negative challenge.

## 10. Flat Prisoner — retain projective topology, clean proof

The 3-D camera matrices, 2-D projected topology, freeze, platform physics,
jumps, and exit contact are independently replayed.  The accepted solver
currently forces a bad freeze, death, thaw, and second freeze solely for smoke
coverage.

The clean trajectory must form a valid topology, freeze once, traverse it, and
escape alive without death or thaw.  A bad projection/death may be tested only
on a separate non-passing challenge.

## Evidence and handoff

For every task, retain distinct initial, active, solved, pass, and real
fail-refresh evidence when the interaction permits it.  Authoritative timed
passes must not contain screenshots or waits that alter the scored timing.
After multi-seed solver, grader-forgery, browser, console, visual, and full
repository checks, all ten remain `pending / human review` for the reviewer to
try by hand. The retained canonical screenshots are supplemented by a second
fresh-seed browser run in temporary evidence: together the two runs provide 20
ordinary-input solves and 60 matching live-server, direct-grader, and
exported-verifier acceptances.

The evidence directory also contains ten 1280×720 solution films in both WebM
and H.264 MP4. Each film records only the clean accepted trajectory against a
live task server. The manifest stores media hashes, three independent grades,
console results, and before/after hashes for the task, verifier, generator,
frontend JS/CSS, shared runtime, grader, and solver. Capture-only pacing makes
short motor actions legible but does not alter benchmark code or thresholds.
